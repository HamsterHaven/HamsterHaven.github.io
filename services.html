Fresh Veggies

Hamsters love crunchy, water-rich veggies that keep them hydrated and healthy. Great options include carrots, broccoli, kale, spinach, and cucumber. Always wash vegetables thoroughly and serve them in small portions to prevent spoilage.

Fresh Fruit

Fruits are a tasty treat and a natural source of vitamins. Safe choices include apples, pears, bananas, and berries. Avoid citrus fruits, as they can upset a hamster’s stomach. Offer fruits only a few times a week due to their sugar content.

Grains and Seeds

Whole grains and seeds give your hamster energy and help keep their teeth healthy. Foods like oats, millet, quinoa, and sunflower seeds are good in moderation. Choose unsalted, natural versions to avoid added fats and sodium.

Proteins

A small amount of protein supports growth and strength. Hamsters can safely eat boiled egg, cooked chicken, mealworms, or tofu. Offer these foods sparingly as a supplement to their regular diet.

Nuts and Legumes

Unsalted nuts such as peanuts, almonds, and walnuts make great occasional treats, while lentils and beans can be offered cooked and cooled. These foods provide healthy fats and fiber but should be given in small portions to avoid weight gain.

Commercial Hamster Mixes

A balanced hamster food mix is the easiest way to meet their daily nutrition needs. These blends usually contain grains, seeds, dried vegetables, and fortified pellets that support dental health and provide a complete diet.
