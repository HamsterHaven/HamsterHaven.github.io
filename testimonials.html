European Hamster

Cricetus cricetus

The largest wild hamster species in Europe, with reddish-brown fur, a black belly, and white patches. It lives in burrows across Central and Eastern Europe and is now endangered due to habitat loss.

Romanian Hamster

Mesocricetus newtoni

Found mainly in Bulgaria and Romania, this medium-sized species has soft brown fur and a pale underside. It prefers dry plains and agricultural areas.

Turkish Hamster

Mesocricetus brandti

Native to southeastern Europe (especially Turkey and nearby regions), this species resembles the Syrian hamster but is smaller and more aggressive.

Greater Long-tailed Hamster

Tscherskia triton

Found in parts of Eastern Europe extending into Asia. It has a longer tail than most hamsters and a distinctive dark stripe down its back.
